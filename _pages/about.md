# About me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Program in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (AOS) at Princeton University, working closely with researchers at NOAA's Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory (GFDL). My current research focuses on evaluating and improving the System for High-resolution prediction on Earth-to-Local Domains (SHiELD) model to understand and forecast high-impact extreme weather events.
My broader research interests lie in the use of high-resolution numerical models to understand and predict extreme weather, with a focus on boundary layer turbulence, tropical cyclone dynamics, land–atmosphere interactions, and urban meteorology. I have worked with various modeling frameworks, including the Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF), Hurricane Analysis and Forecast System (HAFS), and SHiELD models, bridging research and operational systems. I am also interested in applying machine learning methods to improve numerical modeling and understand extreme events.
